Journeys Across Russia

The real country is not in the news releases, but here.

What didn't we see there?

According to VTSIOM polls, 95% of Russians dream of going somewhere, but only 36% plan to spend their holidays in their native country. Like, what haven't we seen here at home? In fact, Russia is a whole universe with the gentle sea of the south, the dense forests of the Sayan Mountains and the harsh ice of the Putorana plateau. And you can also see all these beauties without millions in your account, a passport and many hours of flights. Like, for example, Vera Bashmakova is a brave young mother who took three children in her arms and sat them down in her Lada and I drove 20 thousand kilometers across my native country. We have selected and described some interesting places, worthy of your vacation.

Curonian Spit

URL

park-kosa.ru

Here, amidst forests and sandy dunes, you can see two horizons of water — the calm Curonian Lagoon on one side and the Baltic Sea, rippling with waves, on the other. A unique natural area at the edge of Russia’s enclave.

The Kaliningrad region doesn’t end here. Nearby for the traveler and explorer lies Russia’s westernmost point, the Baltic Spit, along with the German heritage scattered across small seaside towns. The atmosphere in these places is free from hustle, immersing you in the tranquility of nature and the scent of a cool, steel-blue sea.

Kola Peninsula

URL

National Geographic

Almost the entire peninsula is located beyond the Arctic Circle. From the Sami tundra, stretching southward is the taiga, and to the north, the Arctic Ocean disguising itself as the Barents Sea.

Perhaps you’ve watched Zvyagintsev’s films or heard about the Arctic festival in Teriberka. Perhaps the word “Khibiny” hasn’t been buried in the snow of old geography lessons. Maybe you never showed interest in the superdeep borehole piercing Earth’s crust, and apatite minerals might have long evoked apathy in you. But your dream to see the northern lights begins with a ticket to Murmansk.

Altai

URL

YouTube

Altai is one of the most beautiful places in Russia, primarily due to its mountains: if you drive along the ridge, you’ll see slopes dotted with pines, mountain rivers, and lakes. And if you roll down your car windows, you’ll encounter an invisible wonder of these lands — the mountain air.

Altai’s climate is mild, so it’s best to visit in summer. This way, you’ll witness the full diversity of local flora and fauna. Moose roam Altai’s forests, eagles soar over the ridges, and roe deer graze on the plains. And the famous Pallas’s cats also inhabit the Altai region.

Winter Baikal

URL

https://vk.com/baikalmile

Baikal is widely known as the largest lake in the world. Many also know it’s the largest source of fresh water and one of Russia’s most beautiful places.

That’s all true, of course. But Baikal is also an ideal place for skijoring competitions — a sport where a skier ties themselves to a motorcycle, and together they strive to reach maximum speed on ice. In March 2019, a world record was set at the “Baikal Mile” festival — 197.011 km/h.

Karelia

URL

http://vodlozero.ru/

Siberia doesn’t end at the Urals; it ends in Karelia, where the Siberian larch that forms the taiga doesn’t grow west of Vodlozero. Yet here, it soars up to 30 meters tall — the forests of Karelia’s national parks have never known an axe due to impassable swamps. Some pines here are over half a millennium old. Touch a living being that saw the sun long before Ivan the Terrible did. In the primeval forest, there’s no path for hundreds of kilometers. And on the rare trails, trees a couple of meters off the ground bear bear claw marks, letting everyone know who’s boss here.
